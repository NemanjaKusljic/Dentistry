Implants

Implants are alternatives to complex restorative treatment on your natural tooth such as conventional bridges, and dentures. Constructed of Titanium an implant is placed into your jaw bone. Your bone will grow around the implant and osseo-integrate to the fixture. Implant is consisted of three parts: implant body (placed into the jaw), abutment (implantâ€™s part outside the jaw) and the prosthesis (artificial tooth crown).
Dental implants:
- Replace one or more teeth without affecting adjacent teeth.
- Support a bridge and eliminate the need for a removable denture
- Provide support and retention for a denture.
- Make your life quality better (allows you to eat all kinds of food)
Procedure
The placement of a single implant is a relatively straight forward procedure and routinely carried out under local anaesthetic. Many implant procedures require a second stage, once osseo-integration has occurred. Carried out under local anaesthetic the buried implant is uncovered, and impressions taken. In the interim period, while your crown is being made, you will be provided with a provisional restoration. Last stage is to cement the restoration to the implant
How long do the implants last?
Implants can last more than 20 years. However, it also depends on many elements, such as age, diseases, habits, and as the most important, oral hygiene (regular brushing ans flossing), as well as regualr six months check-ups.
